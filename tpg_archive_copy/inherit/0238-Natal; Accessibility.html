A thought as I messed around with the Kinect the other day. Making games "accessible" usually has two not altogether distinct connotations; one being to make games more mass-audience-friendly, and the other to make games playable for those with physical or other disabilities - in essence, the opposite of mass audience.

The Wii was one of the first major efforts that people associate with making games more accessible in the first sense, and now Kinect and Move are making their mark as well, to say nothing of the masses spending their time on social web-games.

The second sense is a bit more tricky

These two connotations are not disparate, as the main purpose of both is to increase the maximum potential audience for the product.

... what does input hardware like this mean for accessibility?
