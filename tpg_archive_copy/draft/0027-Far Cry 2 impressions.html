Far Cry 2 impressions

2009-05-07 14:27:49

status: draft

author: Bryan

sets the game up to be baldur's gate when its actually diablo

sticks too closely to the shooter mold when it hints at something greater

seemingly quick fixes to gameplay that negates the believability of whatever situation preceded it. (buddy rescues you in an awesome sequence, then you drive off without a word more dialogue)

bugs - soldiers shooting at me while facing the wrong direction, not noticing as i drive my car up to them

world believability - can't shoot out a window? can't jump over a fence? can't cut a wire fence?

fire/explosions/rockets/mortars = GOOD

africa makes one think of a place where the natural world still is a major threat to humans. malaria is a start. but in far cry 2 animals seem to exist solely to run in front of your vehicle and die instantly

some pretty interesting/meaningful moral choices at the end of each chapter, unfortunately the rest of the game seems to be largely devoid of them

use of money - would have been interesting if you could bribe people, buy medicine/food, donate to refugees, or needed to buy ammunition rather than finding it everywhere... much greater use of resources could have resulted in a more interesting survival situation. the need for malaria pills was a start in that direction. but became a predictable situation each time.

where... where are the civilians they reference? this game was meant to be an rpg-shooter.
