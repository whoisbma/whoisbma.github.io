On being an 'Idea Guy'

2012-06-09 22:47:16

status: draft

author: Bryan

There's a phrase that game industry folks (I'm guilty of this too) tend to quote with either snark or self-satisfaction whenever the topic of the value of creative ideas in games comes up. "Ideas are cheap - execution is what matters." Despite being an incomplete statement, its still essentially true, and useful to keep perspective on how design, and game design specifically, actually works.

It rarely begins with a complete idea, it isn't problem or solution-focused at its core, and problems and solutions emerge together. A single high or low-level idea, executed well, still isn't enough to form a complete picture, and even a "complete" initial mental model isn't enough to carry a project through while remaining unscathed... at least in my experience. Finally, the most common point, that we've all heard before: everyone has ideas - that doesn't make them good.

The flip side is the argument that ideas may be cheap, but good ideas are worth considerably more. Granted. Moving on.
