junior detectives' brigade - first louis, then rosa
narrative fallacy - something about being human that gives us this inclination. and yet something about it is so satisfying when we're actually proven right. hotel dusk's story is an exercise in extraordinary coincidences of conjoining mysteries.
games have a tendency to telegraph their design - that is show the player that the game was made for them. in short, not a real world. it is rare when a game this linear can actually not feel this way and yet for some reason have all the elements in place that make this so common in other games. here, a mystery unfolds, hunches end up bearing fruit. its because the story is just that well crafted, not about a gameplay element feeling contrived. "can't be a dead-end, that has no purpose, from the design perspective!" - has no meaning here.
